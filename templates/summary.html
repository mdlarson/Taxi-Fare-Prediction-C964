The selected visualizations below will help summarize the taxi fare data behind the model, and better understand the competitive landscape as a whole.

Average Cost by Borough
As expected, we see below in the _____graph_type____ that trips originating in Staten Island are the most expensive, and trips originating in Queens are the least expensive. This is largely a function of distance from LGA.

Top Pickup Locations
In the _____graph_type____ below, note the log scale on the x-axis. Riders in Manhattan make up the largest share of trips, more than all other boroughs combined. (High-cost outliers were excluded from the model, which significantly reduces the number of trips originating in Staten Island in this figure, with benefit of improving overall model accuracy.)

Distribution of Fares
Fares have a two-peaked distribution, with a local maximum around $18-22 and overall maximum at $50–55. The dip in cost is a result of variance in congestion fees and tolls for local trips of similar distance.

Average Cost by Pickup Hour
The _____graph_type____ shows that trip costs increase steadily through the early morning hours, plateau from mid-morning through lunch hours, creat to peak afternoon commute/rush hours, then steeply decline through the evening. There appears to exist an arbitrage opportunity for riders after 9pm and before midnight. Though there is higher variance at those hours, the typical ride costs ~10–20% less than the hours immediately before and after.
