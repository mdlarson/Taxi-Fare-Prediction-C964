Data Summary

The selected visualizations below will help understand the taxi fare data behind the taxi fare prediction model, and the competitive landscape of high-volume, for-hire vehicles in New York City as a whole.

Average Cost by Borough
As expected, we see below in the catplot that trips originating in Staten Island are the most expensive, and trips originating in Queens are the least expensive. This is largely a function of distance from LaGuardia Airport.

Top Pickup Locations
In the barplot below, note the log scale on the x-axis. Riders in Manhattan make up the largest share of trips, more than all other boroughs combined. Extremely high-cost outliers were excluded from the model, which significantly reduces the number of trips originating in Staten Island in this figure, with benefit of improving overall model accuracy.

Distribution of Fares
Fares have a two-peaked distribution, as visible in the histogram below, with a local maximum around $18-22 and overall maximum at $50–55. The dip in cost is often a result of variance in congestion fees and/or tolls for trips of similar distance.

Average Cost by Pickup Hour
The following barplot shows that trip costs increase steadily through the early morning hours, plateau from mid-morning through lunch hours, rise again to the peak afternoon commute/rush hours, then steeply decline through the evening. There is an arbitrage opportunity for riders after 9pm and before midnight, when the typical ride costs ~10–20% less than the hours immediately before and after.

Cost by Hour of Day
The boxplot offers another look at variance and distribution of taxi ride cost over the course of a day. The afternoon hours have the highest median costs, and the greatest variability in fares. Early morning and late night hours tend to be lower in cost, and more consistent.
